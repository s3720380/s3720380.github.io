My Favourite Hobby

I am particularly fond of watching film and television, with an interest in the works of Snyder, Sandler and Jon Favreau. If you were to ask me to mark down my top 5 favourite films i'd say you'd have to be more specific, what genre, which decade did it come out? who stars in it. These are all important questions to consider, however I have from time to time pondered my honest top 5 films and the following list is in no specific order.

1. Shrek/Shrek 2

It is my honest belief that both Shrek 1 and 2 are timeless classics in the long list of movies for children. However the thing that sticks out to me is how anti-disney Shrek 1 is, with many jokes alluding to classic disney characters as well as behaviour entirely un-disney like. For example when Shrek blatantly insults and screams at all the people occupying his home, a moment where in a Disney movie a musical number would probably occur. Overall I would say these are two very strong movies suitable for an audience of any age.

2. The Dark Knight

This film really speaks to me, we truly live in a society.

3. Batman Vs Superman Dawn of Justice

This is a movie that is strongly reminiscent of "Excalibur", in fact during the opening sequence you can see that the theatre in which Bruce and his parents have just exitted is screening Excalibur 1981. When Batman is fighting Superman later in the film it is quite similar to when Arthur is fighting Lancelot, both good men who are fighting due to their idealogical differences, both also eventually reconciling to fight a greater evil. In "Excalibur 1981", Mordred is the son of Arthur and Morgana Le Fay, an unnatural union caused by Morgana's trickery. Similarly in "BvS" Doomsday is caused by the unnatural union of a Kryptonian and a Human, resulting in a monstrous creature hellbent on wreaking chaos and destruction. In "Excalibur 1981" Mordred is ultimately defeated when he is impaled by his father who is also mortally wounded, in "BvS" Doomsday is defeated when he is impaled by Superman who too is mortally wounded. However this is not the only subtext contained within the film. There are various scenes presented which are lifted off of famous artworks for example, after the final conflict when Superman and Wonderwoman are handling Superman's deceased form, the scene is shot in the exact same way as the famous "La descente de croix" a painting from the 16th century. In fact the sequences following this are further allusions to famous classical artists, Cristo Morto & Michelangelo's Pieta. Overall this is a fantastic film with a surprising amount of depth for a modern day superhero movie.

4. American Psycho

This movie introduced me to Huey Lewis and the News who I think really came out into their own with their breakout hit "It's hip to be square" a song I think that any audience can appreciate. In fact the whole album just has a real clear crisp sound, perhaps a result of a budget increase in their recording studio. In any case American Psycho is a fantastic film, I would recommend to anyone who enjoys music.

5. Man of Steel

This is a movie that makes me think back to the works of Plato, specifically "The Republic". The Ideal City is not just a place, but a metaphor for the human soul. In Man of Steel Jor-El imprints the codex within his son. The Codex is Krypton, and Krypton is the Ideal City. Superman has the Ideal City within himself, and he is a perfectly just being. In "The Republic" Plato states that the City is a "city of the mind", an ideal which one can strive towards. Superman is described in this way, as an ideal for humanity to strive towards. The real drama that "Man of Steel" revolves around is Superman having to choose between Krypton (The Ideal City) and Earth (The Imperfect City), and although every cell in Superman's being is Kryptonian, he instead decides to choose the imperfect Earth, hoping for humanity to change for the better. Because in reality Krypton is an artificial utopia, and through greed and corruption the planet is slowly consumed by the sins of its leaders. Overall this was a surprising entry into the superhero genre, with a fantastic soundtrack developed by Hans Zimmer.
