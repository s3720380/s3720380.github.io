My Dream Project

In 2013, Open-World Survival Games flourished within the market, this success continued all the way to 2018, where the genre began to slowly die down in speed. However it is not due to people growing bored of the genre, it is due to the games of this genre being of a below average grade. I have always thought people would be fascinated with an Open-World Survival Game in which the player is thrusted into the Carribean, where they play from the perspective of a hopeful pirate. My thought is further educated by the fact that pirate Open-World games tend to far outsell their competitors; for example, Sea of Thieves surpassed a 5 million concurrent user account just a couple of months after it's release, whilst Conan Exiles another game of the genre has only ever peaked at 53000 concurrent users.

The gameplay would entail the user first constructing a basic raft, one that can simply sail out to nearby ships and then eventually acquiring enough parts and money to construct a more advanced ship capable of attacking larger vessels. The game would allow for users to cooperate through integrated voice chat, text chat and a sophisticated pinging system allowing for precise communication. Being an open world game it only makes sense to allow for users to determine whether they want to be playing against other players or purely against the ai, by allowing for multiple servers users can engage in both PVP and PVE, dependent on what server they're logged onto. By setting it up this way it allows for users to have choice when it comes down to how they want to play the game. Other potential features that could be implemented are, VR integration with the market constantly growing in size and with more and more consumers purchasing VR kits it only makes sense to offer options that allow for the VR gamer to play alongside regular users. If the game ends up being released on Steam or a similar platform that allows for user uploaded content it could be possible to integrate a modding workshop where users can create new content to play with on modded servers. Face-Scanning for more personal character customisation, and a built-in skin editor for weapons and vehicles.

Constructing an Open-World game will not be easy however. Because games of this genre are heavily dependent on having a successful gameplay loop it is integral that this game's loop is unique as opposed to other similar titles and fun for the user to experience. In order to ensure the maximum possible user enjoyment and retention. This could be achieved through making the gameplay mechanics engaging enough to allow for both casual and more dedicated players to keep wanting to log on to play more. These mechanics can come in the form of a rewarding looting system as well as an intuitive hunger system that makes eating and drinking less tedious compared to other titles such as ARK, Minecraft ectAfter release it is important that the game recieves continued update and support to ensure it's userbase can continue growing even as other titles are launched in the genre. another important factor is ensuring that the game is large enough that the players are satisfied with the world they're exploring and contempt with its size. Finally, allowing for customisability lets users personalise their player character and raft enabling them to maintain a distinct identity and allowing for veteran players to flex their in-game wealth and experience. Other important factors to be considered include the products pay model. Wether a freemium model with microtransactions for skins ect is adopted or an initial upfront cost model with less focus on microtransactions, with both having proven success within the genre. Additionally it is important to consider how sustainable the game's model is, perhaps allowing for Users to not only play on licensed servers and modded servers but also pay for their own servers. With these proposed monetisation methods the product can be financially sustainable as well as profitable. Due to the nature of the title it is also important that DLC is released progressively throughout the products lifetime that is not just premium but also free so that users are not alienated by a paywall.

I believe a game of this caliber can be made utilising a well designed commercial engine such as Unity or Unreal. As well as graphical softwares such as Blender, Maya ect. to construct the in-game textures and models. I have chosen these softwares as they've proven to be reliable and allow for heavy user customisation. Other programs that could be potentially beneficial is Adobe Photoshop in constructing textures, as well as promotional material and Adobe Premiere for also creating the latter.

In terms of skill required in order to undertake this project I would say that I would need to pick up either Unity or Unreal in order to construct the game itself and refine my skill at using Maya and Photoshop to create the game's environment. Both Unity and Unreal are open source engines requiring a licensing fee for publishing a game so acquiring them won't necessarily take much effort likewise with Maya and Photoshop (Blender might be a good alternative since it is far cheaper but has far less features compared to Maya). However this is a project that would take far too long to release if undertaken purely by myself, I would most likely need to hire a team of developers to tackle different aspects of the game to ensure it is released in a timely fashion.

If my project is successful and the game is released with all the pre-described features I would hope for the community of Open-World Survival Game enthusiasts to appreciate the entry, and be provided a game that they can play on the regular aside from ARK, Minecraft, Conan Exiles and other popular games of the genre. As well as provide a lasting impression on the industry by demonstrating that whilst the genre may be inflated by clones of ARK and Minecraft, divergent games can still come to be; Ones that are not only fresh but innovative and fun.
